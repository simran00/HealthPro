Paleo Diet

   

The paleo diet is a plant based diet and lean protein diet. These were the foods that were obtained by hunting and gathering during the Paleolithic era. It excludes processed foods such as sugar,dairy, and grains. Some choose this diet because they want to return back to the meal plans of our early ancestors, while some choose paleo because they want to eat fewer calories and lose weight. Humans are not used to the modern way food is produced, and there are processed foods that are hard for many to digest. By going back to the way food used to be eaten, some people find it exxtremely beneficial for their health.

Benefits:

The lack of processed foods in this diet can you feel more energetic, less inflammation, improved heart health, and increased insulin sensitivity. This can lower cholesterol and reduce the risk of heart disease. Other benefits include: improved glucose tolerance, better blood pressure control, lower triglycerides and better appetite management.

Risks:

Some of the risk of this diet is deficiencies in calcium and vitamin D which could lead to issues in bone health. If saturated fat and protein is consumed above recommended levels then it leads to increased risk of kidney, heart disease, and certain cancers. The absence of whole grains and legumes in this diet, means that one can lose a good source of fiber, vitamins and other nutrients. On can also lose sources of portein and calcium due to the lack of dairy products.

Suggested Foods

  • Meat
  • Fish and seafood
  • Eggs
  • vegetables
  • fruits
  • nuts
  • healthy fats
  • oils

Restricted Foods

  • Cereal Grains
  • Legumes (peanuts, beans, lentils, tofu)
  • Refined sugar
  • Processed foods
  • Soda and sweet beverages
  • Refined vegetable oils
  • Salt
  • Artificial sweetners
