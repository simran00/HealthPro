Vegan

   

The Vegan diet is a plant based diet that excludes any animal products. Some choose this diet for ethical or environmental reasons, while some choose veganism to maintain a healthy lifestyle. Vegan diets need to be well planned because some vegans rely a lot on processed foods. These foods are high in calories, sugar, fat and sodium. To plan a beneficial vegan diet, choose healthy plant-based foods and take B-12, calcium and vitamin D supplements.

Benefits: The low fat and high fiber portions in this diet can make you feel fuller for longer. This can lower more body weight and body mass index (BMI) compared to other diets. Vegan diets as well as other plant based diets can reduce risks of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, premature death, Alzheimer's disease and dying from heart disease or cancer.

Risks: Vegan diets are low in vitamin B12, vitamin D, iodine, iron, calcium, zinc and omega-3 fatty acids. Since vegan diets are lower on protien, it can cause blood sugar swings and eating too much carbohydrates.

Suggested Foods:

  • Fruits
  • Vegetables
  • Leafy Greens
  • Whole Grains
  • Nuts
  • Seeds
  • Legumes
  • Plant-Based Milk
